# Can I add a co-applicant?

Yes. A co-applicant is often the difference between approval and rejection, especially when:

- Your individual FOIR is borderline
- You're near the age cap and want a longer tenure
- Your CIBIL is in the 650–720 band
- You're self-employed with one weak ITR year

## Who can be a co-applicant
- Spouse (most common)
- Parent or adult child
- Sibling (case-by-case)
- Business partner (only for self-employed loans)

## How combining works
- **Income** combines (sum of both verified incomes)
- **Existing EMIs** combine (debt of both is counted)
- **CIBIL** uses the **higher** of the two scores for rate-band placement
- **Age** uses the **younger** applicant for tenure calculation

## Things to know
- Both applicants are jointly and severally liable — if either misses payments, both credit reports are hit
- Both must complete KYC and consent to bureau pulls
- Marriage is not required for spouse co-applicant — just shared address proof helps
