# How does tenure affect my EMI?

Tenure is the loan duration in months. Longer tenure means lower monthly EMI but higher total interest paid over the loan's life.

## Rough mental model (₹5L loan at 9% p.a.)
| Tenure | EMI | Total Interest Paid |
|---|---|---|
| 36 months | ₹15,902 | ₹72,464 |
| 48 months | ₹12,440 | ₹97,108 |
| 60 months | ₹10,379 | ₹1,22,727 |
| 72 months | ₹9,011 | ₹1,48,769 |
| 84 months | ₹8,041 | ₹1,75,461 |

Going from 60 → 84 months drops the EMI ~22% but pushes total interest up ~43%.

## How to choose
- **Tightest budget today, growth expected:** longer tenure (you can prepay later when income grows — first 25%/year is free)
- **Comfortable EMI affordable, want lowest cost:** shorter tenure (60 months is the sweet spot for most)
- **Older applicant near age cap:** tenure is decided by policy, not preference
