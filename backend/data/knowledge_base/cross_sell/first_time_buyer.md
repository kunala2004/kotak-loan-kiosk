# Cross-sell fits — First-time car buyers

Most first-time buyers are early in their credit journey. Heavy cross-selling is wrong here — we're playing a long relationship game.

## Modest fits (one of these, max)
- **Entry-level credit card** with low limit — helps build credit history alongside the car loan
- **Term life insurance** — they likely have none, premiums are lowest at this age
- **Two-product family floater health plan** — fits couples buying their first car

## Tone for the brief
Educate, don't pitch. Mention that maintaining the car loan in good standing for 12 months opens a wider product set. The first car loan is often the customer's first credit experience that scales — get it right and they'll come back for the home loan.

## Avoid
- Personal loans (their CIBIL is too thin)
- Big-ticket cross-sell (overwhelming)
- Insurance bundles where they don't see what they're paying for separately

## Long game
Track when their first 12 EMIs are paid cleanly — that's the moment to offer pre-approved upgrades.
