# Soft flag thresholds (note in brief, not auto-escalated)

These are signals that don't justify rejection on their own but should be visible to the underwriter / dealer in the brief.

## Soft signals (1–2 of these = note, 3+ = manual review)
- 1–2 bounced transactions in last 6 months of bank statements
- Income consistency 15–25% (above 25% becomes a hard flag)
- Existing EMI burden 35–45% of income before adding the new car EMI
- Recent hard inquiries in bureau (> 3 in last 90 days)
- Salary credit pattern broken in last 2 months (could be job change)
- Multiple addresses across documents (could be legitimate move)
- Overdrafts used > 80% in last 3 months
- Credit utilisation > 60% on revolving credit

## How to phrase soft flags in the brief
Use neutral language ("3 hard inquiries in last 90 days — may indicate active credit shopping; not blocking") not alarmist language ("multiple credit pulls suggest financial distress"). The dealer will see this and may ask the customer about it conversationally.

Soft flags are about transparency, not gatekeeping. The decision is still based on the rules engine output.
